Our mission and values

What we found as a problem.

Loneliness is a pressing issue in university. In 2017, 63.1% of nearly 48,000 college students indicated that they had felt “very lonely” in the previous 12 months [1]. This phenomenon is also prevalent in us. “In a large university bigger than my village, making friends is hard as hell,” says a Berkeley student who would like to keep anonymous. 

What we spotted as a solution.

We encounter 3 million faces on average in our lifetime. The study shows that people tend to underestimate the benefits brought by conversing with strangers.

That's how Fifteen came to life!

Fifteen aims to leverage the unexplored potential of with-stranger conversations to improve the well-being of students and reduce loneliness on college campuses. We aspire to bring college students closer in an adventurous way and cultivate a student community with tighter bonds.

Fifteen

Fifteen is a platform that connects university students and encourages them to meet up with a stranger for fifteen minutes every day. We use our algorithm to pair up our users to a stranger and exchange basic information. They first chat with each other online anonymously, and can decide if they want to spend fifteen minutes together in real life.

We also create visualizations of people’s connections for our user, giving them the first-hand insight on how their community is bonded together.
